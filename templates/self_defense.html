Self-defense is not about fighting, it is about **awareness, confidence, and quick decision-making**. Learning basic self-defense techniques empowers women to protect themselves and respond effectively in dangerous situations.

Stay Alert
Always be aware of your surroundings, especially in isolated or crowded places. Avoid distractions like excessive phone usage.
Trust Your Instincts
If a situation or person feels unsafe, trust your gut feeling. Your instincts are often the first warning sign of danger.
Use Your Voice
Shout loudly to attract attention and scare the attacker. Confidence and loud voice can discourage harmful actions.
Target Weak Points
Aim for sensitive areas like eyes, nose, throat, or groin to escape quickly if physical defense becomes necessary.
Use Available Objects
Everyday items like keys, bags, umbrellas, or water bottles can be used as defensive tools in emergencies.
Escape & Seek Help
Your primary goal is to escape safely. Run to crowded areas and contact emergency services immediately.
